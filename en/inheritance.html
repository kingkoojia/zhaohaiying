Cross-Border Inheritance

Protecting your inheritance rights across borders

Overview

Cross-border inheritance involves foreign nationals or overseas Chinese inheriting assets located in China. Common scenarios include children who have moved abroad inheriting parents' Chinese properties, or foreign nationals entitled to inherit from a Chinese estate.

Attorney Zhao Haiying has extensive experience handling cross-border inheritance cases efficiently, covering inheritance notarization, real estate registration, equity transfer, and bank deposit withdrawal.

Typical Clients

Overseas Chinese / Naturalized Citizens — Inheriting real estate and other assets in China while living abroad, unable to handle procedures in person.

Foreign Nationals — Entitled to inherit from a Chinese national's estate under Chinese inheritance law.

Non-Resident Heirs — Need to sell inherited property and transfer the proceeds abroad.

Services

  • Inheritance Strategy — Tailored inheritance plan based on your specific circumstances
  • Inheritance Notarization — Full representation in obtaining inheritance notarial certificate
  • Real Estate Registration — Property title transfer at the local real estate registry
  • Equity & Securities Transfer — Transfer of company shares and securities accounts
  • Bank Asset Withdrawal — Release of bank deposits and financial products
  • Asset Liquidation & Repatriation — Sale of inherited assets and cross-border fund transfer

Process

  1. Determine Inheritance Method — Statutory or testamentary succession
  2. Document Collection — Death certificate, kinship proof, asset documents
  3. Inheritance Notarization — Apply at a Chinese notary office (remotely available)
  4. Asset Registration — Register title change at relevant government office
  5. Fund Repatriation (if needed) — Tax clearance and cross-border remittance

Remote Representation

You do not need to travel to China. The process works seamlessly: execute a power of attorney at your local notary, have it authenticated (or apostilled), and send it to us. We handle all in-country procedures on your behalf.
